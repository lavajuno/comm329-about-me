About Me

Introduction

My name is Juno,

I am a senior in computer science at Clarkson University. I was born and raised in central Massachusetts, where I played in my high school's marching band, ran cross country, and was on the local FIRST robotics team.

My interest in computers started in middle school when I wanted to learn how to run a Minecraft server. I ended up programming for my high school's robotics team for a couple years before choosing to come to Clarkson.

What I've been up to

My time at Clarkson has been jam packed. I ran a show at our radio station for a while, and joined a bunch of clubs.

Nowadays, I help run the Clarkson Open Source Institute, the amateur radio club, and the gender/sexuality alliance. While at Clarkson, I have done some web development for the CS labs, as well as a couple personal projects.

In Summer 2024, I started working for Mission Sun, a startup designing utility-scale transformers and solar inverters. At Mission Sun, I do a lot of full-stack development, specifically for our parts database and manufacturing systems. I also write plugins for our ERP system to help us manage our inventory and manufacturing line.

Why I'm taking COMM 329

I chose to take this course as part of my new IT minor, and also because of my personal interest in the subject. As the continuation of Principles of UX Design, I hope that it will help me design powerful and intuitive software down the road. As a standalone frontend development course, I hope that it will help me pick up and develop new and existing skills to create full-featured applications.
